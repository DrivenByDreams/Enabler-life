Our mission

Accessibility is not an afterthought.

At Enabler, we believe that technology built without accessibility in mind is technology built for fewer people. Our work starts with the people who are too often left out of the design process.

We partner with occupational therapists, disability advocates, and users with lived experience to build tools that genuinely fit into people's lives — not tools people have to adapt around.

Co-designed, always

Every product is built in active collaboration with the community it serves.

Affordable by design

Assistive technology should not be a luxury. We keep cost at the center of our decisions.

Open and interoperable

Our solutions are built to work with existing devices and ecosystems.

Our solutions

What we build

A growing portfolio of assistive tools designed around real user needs.

Vision

SmartSight Navigator

A low-cost wearable that uses ultrasonic sensing and haptic feedback to help visually impaired users navigate indoor and outdoor environments independently.

Learn more →
Communication

TalkBoard Lite

An open-source AAC (augmentative and alternative communication) board running on low-cost microcontrollers, giving non-verbal individuals a portable voice anywhere.

Learn more →
Motor

GripAssist Glove

A sensor-equipped assistive glove with servo-actuated finger support, enabling users with limited hand mobility to perform daily tasks with greater independence.

Learn more →
Smart Home

HomeReach Hub

An accessible smart-home controller with switch-access, voice, and eye-gaze input modes — compatible with existing IoT ecosystems and requiring no app setup.

Learn more →
Cognitive

DailyFlow Planner

A simplified, symbol-based daily routine assistant for individuals with cognitive or learning disabilities — designed to reduce anxiety and build independence.
